Screen Reader

Empowering visually impaired individuals to navigate digital content effortlessly.

What is a Screen Reader?

A screen reader is an assistive technology that converts text displayed on a screen into speech or braille. It is primarily used by individuals who are blind or have severe visual impairments to interact with computers, smartphones, and other devices.

How Does a Screen Reader Work?

  • Reads aloud the content of web pages, documents, and applications.
  • Provides keyboard shortcuts to navigate without using a mouse.
  • Interprets and communicates text, images (via alt text), and interactive elements like buttons or forms.

Benefits of Screen Readers

  1. Enhances digital accessibility for visually impaired individuals.
  2. Promotes independence in education, work, and daily life.
  3. Improves access to online information, shopping, and communication platforms.

Popular Screen Readers

Here are some widely-used screen readers:

  • JAWS (Job Access With Speech)
  • NVDA (NonVisual Desktop Access)
  • VoiceOver (for macOS and iOS)
  • TalkBack (for Android)
