Employment Information for People with Disabilities

Access your rights, job opportunities, and helpful resources

Welcome to Employment Information for People with Disabilities

This page provides essential resources, information on employment rights, and support for individuals with disabilities seeking or maintaining employment. Whether you're just starting your career or looking for better workplace accommodations, this guide offers valuable insights.

Your Employment Rights

As an individual with a disability, you are entitled to certain legal protections to ensure fair treatment in the workplace. Some of the key rights include:

  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA): Protects individuals from discrimination in the workplace and requires employers to provide reasonable accommodations.
  • Equal Employment Opportunity: Employers cannot discriminate based on disability, and they must provide equal opportunities for hiring, promotion, and benefits.
  • Reasonable Accommodations: Employers are required to make adjustments to the work environment or work processes to accommodate the needs of employees with disabilities.
  • Right to Accessible Facilities: Employers must ensure that their facilities are accessible, including physical access to buildings, software, and communications.
  • Protection from Retaliation: You are protected from retaliation if you assert your rights under disability laws.

Job Opportunities for Individuals with Disabilities

Many employers actively seek to hire individuals with disabilities and provide supportive work environments. Here are some ways to find job opportunities:

  • Job Boards for Disability Employment: Websites like DisabilityJobs.org and ODEP (Office of Disability Employment Policy) offer resources and job listings tailored to individuals with disabilities.
  • Employer Incentives: Many employers offer incentives, such as tax credits, for hiring people with disabilities. Explore these opportunities when applying.
  • Supportive Employment Programs: Government programs and non-profit organizations provide job training, placement services, and ongoing support for individuals with disabilities.
  • Internships and Apprenticeships: Some employers offer internships or apprenticeships designed to give people with disabilities hands-on experience in various industries.
