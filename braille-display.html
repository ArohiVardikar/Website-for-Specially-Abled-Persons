Voice and Braille Display Technology

Bridging the gap for visually impaired individuals through tactile and auditory communication.

What is Voice and Braille Display Technology?

Voice and Braille displays are assistive technologies designed for individuals with visual impairments. They provide information in both auditory and tactile formats, enabling users to access digital content seamlessly.

How Does It Work?

Voice Technology: Converts on-screen text into speech, allowing users to listen to digital content.

Braille Display: A device that translates on-screen text into tactile braille characters using a refreshable braille display.

  • Uses text-to-speech (TTS) engines for auditory output.
  • Employs movable braille pins to represent text dynamically.
  • Works with screen readers and other assistive software for navigation and interaction.

Benefits of Voice and Braille Displays

  1. Enables access to digital content for visually impaired individuals.
  2. Supports education and professional development by providing alternative reading formats.
  3. Enhances independence in navigating computers
